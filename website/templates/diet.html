You must obey the laws of thermodynamics!

Calories-in, calories-out (CICO) is a fundamental principle in weight management that focuses on the balance between energy intake and energy expenditure.

The concept is based on the idea that to maintain, gain, or lose weight, the number of calories consumed through food and beverages must align with the number of calories burned through physical activity and metabolism. When individuals consume more calories than they burn, the excess energy is stored as body fat, leading to weight gain.

Conversely, if individuals consume fewer calories than they expend, the body utilizes stored fat as an energy source, resulting in weight loss. While CICO provides a foundation for weight management, it is important to consider the quality of calories consumed and the overall nutritional balance of one's diet for optimal health and well-being.

A good rule of thumb is the following:
If you eat more calories than you burn, you will put on weight.
If you eat more calories than you burn, and eat sufficient protein, and train, you will put on muscle.
If you eat less calories than you burn, you will lose weight.
If you eat less calories than you burn, and eat sufficient protein, and train, you will lose fat while maintaining muscle.


The recommended protein intake is 0.6 to 1.1g of protein per pound of bodyweight daily.
